Come leave the noise of the world behind for a morning of movement, music, and pure joy in the beauty of Mt. Madonna County Park.

A Sacred Hike & Sound Gathering is a joyful, accessible outdoor experience for all ages and all walks of life. No spiritual background required. No experience needed. Just bring your body, your voice, and your appetite.

We begin with an easy to moderate hike along one of Mt. Madonna's gorgeous trails, winding through towering redwoods and oak woodland. Then we sit at picnic tables and share a communal meal together. Bring your own food, drinks, and snacks — there is something deeply nourishing about eating together outdoors in the company of good people.

After we eat, we settle in for a sound journey with Mark and Lynetta. Mark, a spiritual Rabbi who loves the joy of sacred music, leads us in niggunim — the simple, soulful Hebrew melodies made famous by Rabbi Shlomo Carlebach. These are wordless, repetitive, deeply joyful songs that anyone can sing. No Hebrew required. Just open your mouth and let the sound come through you.

Lynetta brings her Shruti box, crystal bowls, and Tibetan bowls, weaving together a gentle soundscape in the timeless wisdom that sound is a pathway to stillness and inner awareness. Spontaneous, wordless tones emerge through breath and intuition — your voice becoming another instrument if you choose.

Together we create a living sound experience that invites relaxation, awareness, and deeper connection with yourself and the natural world around you. This is a day for your whole self. Move your body. Lift your voice. Feed your soul.

What to expect:

  • An easy to moderate hike through towering redwoods and oak woodland
  • A communal picnic lunch together at the park
  • Niggunim — joyful, wordless Hebrew melodies led by Mark
  • A sound journey with Shruti box, crystal bowls, and Tibetan bowls
  • An open, spontaneous sound experience — your voice is welcome

What to bring:

  • Comfortable walking shoes and layers (weather can change)
  • Plenty of water
  • Food, drinks, and snacks for your communal lunch
  • Something comfortable to sit on for the sound gathering
  • A wide open heart

Parking:

There is a $6 per vehicle parking fee payable at the ranger station upon entry to Mt. Madonna County Park. Please bring a credit card in case there is no ranger on site — you will use a pay station. Meeting point: the Amphitheater Parking area.

What to Know Before You Come

Where do we meet?

Meet at the Amphitheater Parking area inside Mt. Madonna County Park, 7850 Pole Line Road, Watsonville, CA 95076. Doors open at 8:45 AM.
