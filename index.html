A Way Home

So many of us have forgotten what it feels like to live in our bodies.

We sit at screens for eight, ten hours a day, under fluorescent light, in Silicon Valley's endless hum, disconnected from sun, from soil, from the sound of birds underneath the traffic. We live in our heads because that's what the world asked of us. But our hearts, our wild animal bodies, our connection to each other and to Mother Earth herself, are still there, waiting.

Circle of Loving Kindness is a way home. Back into your body. Back into your heart. Back into community. Back into your true nature, and hers.

The Ceremony

What Happens in the Circle

Movement

Spiral Dance

To music that moves through Organic Downtempo, World Fusion, and Sacred Bass, we spiral together in circle. No one standing out, no one performing. Every step becomes a prayer, sent out as a wave of Loving Kindness touching all our relations, everywhere.

Feeling

Emotional Dance

We dance each emotion as it moves through us: Anger, Sadness, Joy, Grief, Peace, Fear, Love. Nothing rehearsed, nothing choreographed. Your voice is invited to sound exactly as it needs to. All feelings are welcome here.

Sound

Community Sound

Rattles, drums, chimes, and Tibetan bowls. Everyone playing, everyone part of the sound. No experience necessary. This is not a performance. This is us, together, as one instrument.

The Heart of Our Circle

Toning

Our voices rise together, rooted in the Buddhist practice of Metta, loving-kindness meditation. We start on a shared OM, then let our own sounds emerge: imperfect, unrehearsed, true. This is where the blessing becomes sound. May you be well, may you be happy, may you be loved.

About Mark & Lynetta

We Know What It Feels Like to Come Home to Yourself.

We've been together fourteen years, married thirteen, as of June 2nd, 2026.

When we found each other, we were both carrying a childhood that had taught us fear instead of safety. We didn't know it yet, but we were about to give each other something neither of us had ever fully had: a place to finally put it down. He became my sanctuary. I became his. Slowly, in that safety, we each began to heal.

That's the real root of everything we do together now — whether it's toning, dancing, or holding space for a room full of strangers finding their way back into their bodies. We know what it feels like to come home to yourself after believing, for a long time, that you couldn't. We want that for you too.
